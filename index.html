Hi! I'm Amartya. I'm an HPC Software Engineer at Calligo Technologies. I completed my MSc in High Performance Computing from EPCC, University of Edinburgh. I work at the intersection of high-performance computing and computational physics — implementing, profiling, and comparing parallel solvers, CFD kernels, and numerical methods across conventional x86 architectures and experimental RISC-V hardware with posit arithmetic. My work spans everything from multigrid thermal solvers to Lattice Boltzmann Method kernels, with deep profiling across memory, cache, power, and roofline performance. I also have extensive experience in implementing scientific computing kernels on the Cerebras Wafer-Scale Engine (WSE-3) and GPU programming (CUDA).

I'm passionate high-performance computing, computational physics and mathematics, open-source software, linguistics, socio-political issues, and the intersection of technology and society. When I'm not coding, you'll find me hiking, reading, or working out.

Some of my research

  • Multi-Material Thermal Analysis using HYPRE BoomerAMG using x86 (IEEE 754) and RISC-V(UTTUNGA with Posits) As part of my work at Calligo Technologies, I implemented a heat transfer model for a composite 2-D material of aluminium and carbon-fibre reinforced polymer using the Poisson equation in HYPRE BoomerAMG and visualised the results. This was done for x86 (with IEEE 754 floats) and RISC-V (using Calligo's UTTUNGA board with Posits). The x86 version failed to converge for extremely low error tolerances in the range of 1e-14 to 1e-15, whereas the UTTUNGA version converged in around 11-12 iterations for the same. This can be credited to the tapered precision offered by Posits around the golden range of 1.0. This experiment proved how well suited Uttunga with Posits is for such high-fidelity scientific computations.
  • Lattice-Boltzmann methods for Computational Fluid Dynamics on Cerebras CS-3
    • As part of my MSc project under the supervision of Dr. Justs Zarins at EPCC, I worked on implementing the Taylor Green Vortex Decay problem using Lattice-Boltzmann methods on the Cerebras CS3 accelerator. While this problem has been previously implemented in parallel using OpenMP, MPI, and CUDA for Nvidia GPUs, a similar implementation for the Cerebras CS3 is yet to be achieved.
